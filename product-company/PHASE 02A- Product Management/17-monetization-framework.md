# monetization-framework.md

> **Skill:** Produces a monetization framework that maps the full range of monetization options for a product, evaluates them against strategic criteria, and provides a prioritized recommendation.

---

## TLDR

A monetization framework systematically evaluates how a product can generate revenue: subscription, usage, marketplace, data, services, advertising, and more. It documents the options, evaluates them against strategic and financial criteria, and recommends a primary and secondary monetization model. Used when launching a product, pivoting a business model, or evaluating monetization diversification.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Monetization Models for SaaS and Product Companies

**Subscription:** Recurring fee for access. Predictable revenue. Best for products with ongoing value delivery.

**Usage-based:** Pay for what you consume. Revenue scales with customer success. Less predictable.

**Freemium:** Free base tier with paid upgrades. Reduces sales friction. Requires clear upgrade path. Can cannibalize paid conversion.

**Marketplace fees:** Take rate on transactions between buyers and sellers. Scales with ecosystem activity. Requires two-sided network.

**Data monetization:** Aggregate anonymized data into insights, benchmarks, or reports sold to buyers. High margin. Requires trust and consent.

**Professional services:** Implementation, training, consulting. Improves adoption. Low margin, limits scale.

**Add-ons and expansion:** Base product free or low-cost, revenue from add-on features. Reduces initial purchase friction. Requires clear add-on value.

### The Right Model Depends on Value Creation

Monetization models should align with how value is created. If value is delivered continuously (ongoing data, continuous compliance monitoring), subscription aligns well. If value is delivered at specific events (transactions processed, reports generated), usage-based aligns better. Misaligned monetization (subscription for a product that delivers one-time value) creates customer resistance.

## Output Format

```
1. MONETIZATION CONTEXT
   What the product does and how value is created for customers.
   Current monetization model (if any) and its limitations.
   Strategic goals monetization must serve.

2. MONETIZATION OPTIONS EVALUATED
   For each relevant model:
   - Model description
   - How it would apply to this product
   - Revenue potential: high / medium / low
   - Customer alignment: does this feel fair to customers?
   - Operational complexity
   - Best suited to: [company stage, customer type, product maturity]

3. EVALUATION CRITERIA
   Strategic fit: alignment with product vision and value creation
   Revenue predictability
   Customer acceptance and willingness to pay
   Competitive precedent
   Operational feasibility

4. RECOMMENDED PRIMARY MODEL
   The primary monetization model with rationale.
   How it aligns with value creation.
   Revenue model projections.

5. RECOMMENDED SECONDARY MODELS
   Additional monetization layers that complement the primary.
   Sequencing: which to add when.

6. MONETIZATION RISKS
   Risks in the recommended model.
   Mitigation strategies.

7. TRANSITION PLAN (if changing models)
   How to move from current to recommended model.
   Customer impact and communication.
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product manager developing a monetization framework.

Product: [what the product does]
Value creation: [how and when customers experience value]
Customer type: [B2B / B2C, size, segment]
Current monetization: [how you currently make money, if at all]
Competitive context: [how competitors monetize]
Strategic goals: [what monetization must achieve]

Evaluate all relevant monetization options and produce a recommendation
for primary and secondary models with rationale and transition plan.
```
