# press-release.md

> **Skill:** Produces a product or company press release in AP style with inverted pyramid structure, for media distribution, newsroom publication, and internal strategic clarity.

---

## TLDR

A press release is the authoritative announcement document for a product launch, funding event, partnership, or significant milestone. Written in AP style with an inverted pyramid structure: most newsworthy information first, supporting detail following. Used externally for journalists and analysts, and internally as the Amazon PR/FAQ forcing function that clarifies what the actual news is before any execution begins.

---

## Topic Explanation

### The Amazon PR/FAQ Method

Amazon requires product teams to write the press release before building the product. The discipline forces clarity: if you cannot write a compelling press release, you may not have a compelling product or a clearly understood value proposition. Even used as an internal document, writing a draft press release forces the team to answer: what is the actual news, why does it matter, and who should care enough to act?

Teams that struggle to write the lead paragraph have not yet achieved clarity on what they are launching.

### AP Style Essentials

- Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
- Use "percent" not "%" in body text (exception: financial tables)
- Dates: Month Day, Year (January 15, 2026). Abbreviate months except May, June, July
- Company names: exact legal name on first reference
- Titles: capitalize before a name ("Chief Executive Officer Jane Smith"), lowercase after ("Jane Smith, chief executive officer")
- Attribution: use "said," not "says" — press releases use past tense attribution
- Quotes must add insight or perspective. They must never simply restate what the preceding paragraph already said in direct speech.

### Inverted Pyramid

Lead paragraph: the most newsworthy fact. Who did what, when, where, and why it matters. No setup, no preamble, no "we are excited to announce."

Second paragraph: the most important supporting context, plus the first executive quote.

Body paragraphs: supporting evidence, secondary quotes, customer validation, product detail.

Boilerplate: standard company description. Always the final element before media contact.

---

## Dos

- **Do write the lead paragraph so it could stand alone as the complete story.** If a journalist reads only the first paragraph, the essential news should be conveyed.
- **Do make quotes add genuine insight.** The best press release quotes express a conviction, a perspective, or an implication that the surrounding text does not state. "This changes how finance teams work" is more quotable than "We are pleased to offer this solution."
- **Do include a customer quote in product launch releases wherever possible.** Customer voice is more credible than company voice for product claims.

---

## Donts

- **Dont use marketing language in press release copy.** "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "industry-leading," and "best-in-class" will be cut by every editor. Use specific facts instead.
- **Dont bury the news.** The inverted pyramid structure puts the most important fact first. The company name, founding story, and vision belong in the boilerplate, not the lead.

## Output Format

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[or]
EMBARGOED UNTIL: [Day, Month Date, Year at Time Time zone]

[HEADLINE: Active voice. Present tense. Specific. 10 words or fewer.]
[SUBHEAD (optional): One supporting sentence adding context to the headline]

[DATELINE: CITY, State, Month Date, Year] -- [Company name], [brief company descriptor in
5 to 8 words], today announced [the news stated as clearly and specifically as possible].

"[Executive quote: 2 to 3 sentences expressing genuine insight or conviction about the news.
Must not restate the preceding paragraph. Should sound like a person, not a press release.]"
said [Full Name], [Title], [Company name].

[Second paragraph: the most important supporting context. Who benefits, how, and why this matters
to the market. Make the business significance clear without marketing language.]

[Third paragraph: product detail, customer validation, or market context as appropriate.
Include a customer quote here if available.]

"[Customer quote: what the product means for their business specifically, in their language.]"
said [Full Name], [Title], [Customer company name].

[Fourth paragraph: availability, pricing, where to learn more. One clear call to action.]

###

About [Company Name]
[3 to 5 sentences. What the company does, who it serves, brief scale or traction indicators,
founding year, website. No marketing language. Factual and specific.]
[company website]

Media Contact:
[Full Name]
[Title]
[Email address]
[Phone number]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketing manager writing a press release.

Announcement type: [product launch / funding round / partnership / company milestone]
The news in one sentence: [what is actually being announced]
Company name and descriptor: [name and 5 to 8 word description]
Why this matters: [the significance to customers and the market]
Executive name, title, and quote direction: [name, title, and the message they want to convey]
Customer name, title, company, and quote (if available): [or note if none available]
Availability and pricing: [when and at what price point]
Media contact: [name, title, email, phone]
Release timing: [immediate / embargoed until specific date and time]

Write a press release in AP style.
The lead paragraph must contain the most newsworthy fact.
The headline must be 10 words or fewer in active voice.
Executive and customer quotes must add insight not already stated in surrounding text.
No marketing superlatives: use specific facts instead.
```
