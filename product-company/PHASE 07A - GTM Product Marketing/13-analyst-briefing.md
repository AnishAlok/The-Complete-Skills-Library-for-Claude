# analyst-briefing.md

> **Skill:** Produces an analyst briefing document for Gartner, Forrester, IDC, or similar research firms covering market view, product overview, roadmap, and customer traction.

---

## TLDR

An analyst briefing document prepares an AR team and executives for a formal briefing with a research analyst. It structures the story the company wants to tell, anticipates evaluation criteria, and organizes the evidence needed to influence analyst perception, research positioning, and market reports including Magic Quadrant, Wave, and MarketScape evaluations.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Analyst Relations Matters

Gartner Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, and IDC MarketScape placements directly influence enterprise buying decisions. Procurement teams evaluating a new platform often start with analyst research. Companies positioned as leaders or strong performers in relevant analyst research have a demonstrable pipeline advantage.

Analyst briefings are not sales calls. The wrong approach: treat the briefing as a product demo. The right approach: treat it as a research conversation where you educate the analyst on your market view and provide compelling evidence of execution.

### What Analysts Evaluate

Most frameworks evaluate two dimensions:

Vision/Strategy: Where is the market going? Does the company have a credible and differentiated view of the future?

Execution/Completeness of offering: Can the company deliver on its vision today? What customer evidence demonstrates traction?

Briefing materials must address both dimensions with specific evidence, not marketing language.

## Output Format

```
ANALYST BRIEFING DOCUMENT

Briefing target: [analyst name, firm, research coverage area]
Briefing date: [date]
Briefing type: [initial briefing / update / Magic Quadrant / Wave inclusion response]
Presenters: [names and titles]
NDA required: [yes / no]
Prepared by: [AR lead and PMM]

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW (5 minutes)
   Founding year, HQ, employee count.
   Funding stage and total raised (or public company status).
   ARR range or revenue range if shareable under NDA.
   Customer count and notable names if permitted.

2. OUR MARKET VIEW (10 minutes)
   The problem we address and why it has become urgent.
   How the market is evolving and where it is going.
   Why this is an inflection point.
   Our category definition and why it is the correct frame.
   [Must express a distinct, defensible perspective, not generic trend observation]

3. PRODUCT OVERVIEW (15 minutes)
   What the platform does and for whom.
   Architecture at a high level.
   Key differentiating capabilities vs. market alternatives.
   Major releases in the last 12 months.

4. PRODUCT ROADMAP (10 minutes)
   [Present at high level unless under NDA]
   Strategic themes for next 12 to 18 months (not specific features).
   How the roadmap serves the stated market vision.

5. CUSTOMER TRACTION (10 minutes)
   Customer count and YoY growth rate.
   Representative use cases (anonymized if necessary).
   2 to 3 specific customer stories with measurable outcomes.
   Retention and expansion metrics (NRR if shareable).

6. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING
   How we see the competitive landscape.
   Where we compete and where we intentionally do not.
   Our view on how evaluation criteria should be applied in this market.

7. ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS AND PREPARED RESPONSES
   For each question the analyst is likely to ask:
   Question: [anticipated question]
   Response: [prepared, specific, honest answer]

8. DESIRED OUTCOMES
   What perception shift this briefing aims to achieve.
   Which research this should influence.
   Follow-up materials to be sent after the briefing.
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are an analyst relations lead preparing a briefing document.

Analyst and firm: [name and firm]
Briefing purpose: [initial / update / MQ or Wave inclusion]
Company overview: [size, funding, founding year]
Market view: [the company's distinct perspective on the market]
Product capabilities: [what the platform does]
Roadmap themes: [strategic direction at high level]
Customer traction data: [metrics and stories]
Competitive landscape view: [how the company sees competition]

Produce a complete analyst briefing document with anticipated Q&A.
Market view must express a distinct and arguable perspective, not trend recitation.
Customer traction must include specific metrics.
Anticipated questions must be realistic analyst challenges, not softballs.
```
