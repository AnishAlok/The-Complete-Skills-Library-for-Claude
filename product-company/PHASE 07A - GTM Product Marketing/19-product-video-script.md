# product-video-script.md

> **Skill:** Produces a product demo or explainer video script with scene descriptions, voiceover copy, and on-screen text for a 60 to 180-second product video.

---

## TLDR

A product video script defines exactly what the viewer sees, hears, and reads on screen for a product demo or explainer video. This skill produces a fully formatted two-column script: scene descriptions with timing, voiceover narration, and on-screen text. Used by video production teams to plan, shoot, and edit a demo, explainer, or feature spotlight video.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Video Type Selection

**Explainer video (60 to 90 seconds):** Typically animated or motion graphics. Explains what the product does at a category level. No screen recording. Used at the top of the funnel to create awareness and category understanding.

**Demo video (90 to 180 seconds):** Screen recording with voiceover. Shows the actual product working. Used in evaluation stages. Must show the product solving a real problem, not just clicking through features.

**Feature spotlight (30 to 60 seconds):** Focused screen recording showing one specific capability. Used in email announcements and in-app notifications.

### The 90-Second Rule

Viewer drop-off on product videos increases sharply after 90 seconds. An 80% retention rate at 90 seconds drops below 50% at 3 minutes for most B2B product videos. A 90-second explainer that lands the core message is more effective than a 3-minute comprehensive walkthrough. If a demo needs 3 minutes, the first 30 seconds must earn that commitment.

### Script Formatting

The two-column format lets the production team plan visual direction and audio simultaneously. Visual direction (left) describes what appears on screen. Audio (right) covers voiceover and any on-screen text overlays.

## Output Format

```
VIDEO SCRIPT

Title: [Product - Video type]
Target duration: [60 / 90 / 120 / 180 seconds]
Video type: [Explainer / Demo / Feature spotlight]
Audience: [who will watch this]
Goal: [what the viewer should think, feel, or do after watching]
Visual style: [animation / screen recording / live action / mixed]

PRODUCTION NOTES
Voiceover tone: [professional / conversational / energetic]
Music direction: [upbeat / understated / none]
Key brand requirements: [logo placement, color usage, etc.]

---

SCRIPT

[SCENE 1 - 00:00 to 00:08] [HOOK]
VISUAL: [Description of what appears on screen]
VO: "[Exact voiceover words]"
ON SCREEN: [Any text overlay. "None" if clean.]

[SCENE 2 - 00:08 to 00:20] [PROBLEM]
VISUAL: [Description]
VO: "[Voiceover]"
ON SCREEN: [Text or None]

[SCENE 3 - 00:20 to 00:30] [INTRODUCE PRODUCT]
VISUAL: [Description]
VO: "[Voiceover]"
ON SCREEN: [Product name or tagline]

[Continue for each scene through demo, proof, and CTA]

[FINAL SCENE - CTA]
VISUAL: [Product logo. Website URL.]
VO: "[Specific call to action with URL]"
ON SCREEN: "[URL]" and CTA text

---

TIMING SUMMARY
Scene 1 (Hook): 00:00 to 00:08
Scene 2 (Problem): 00:08 to 00:20
Scene 3 (Introduce product): 00:20 to 00:30
[Continue]
Total runtime: [X seconds]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketer writing a product video script.

Video type: [explainer / demo / feature spotlight]
Target duration: [60 / 90 / 120 / 180 seconds]
Target audience: [who will watch this]
Opening hook: [the problem or situation to open with]
Product introduction: [how the product enters the narrative]
Key capabilities to show (2 to 3): [what to demonstrate]
Proof element: [customer quote or metric to include if any]
Call to action: [what the viewer should do after watching]
Visual style: [animation / screen recording / mixed]

Write a complete video script with scene-by-scene breakdown,
voiceover copy, on-screen text, and visual direction for each scene.
The hook must appear in the first 8 seconds.
The product must be introduced by 25% of total runtime.
The CTA must be clear and specific in the final 10 seconds.
```
