# landing-page-copy.md

> **Skill:** Produces a landing page copy framework with hero section, value proposition blocks, social proof, feature sections, objection handling, and CTA blocks for a product or campaign page.

---

## TLDR

A landing page copy framework defines every copy block on a product or campaign landing page: the hero headline and subhead, value sections, social proof elements, feature/benefit blocks, objection-handling copy, and CTAs. This skill produces the full copy hierarchy that a designer and developer can build from, optimized for multiple reading speeds.

---

## Topic Explanation

### The Three Reading Speeds

Landing page copy must work at three levels because different visitors scan at different speeds:

**Scan (8 seconds):** Headline, subhead, section headers, bold text, CTAs. The visitor who barely lands gets the core message. If the scan message fails, 80% of visitors leave.

**Skim (30 to 60 seconds):** First sentence of each paragraph, bullet points, pull quotes. The engaged visitor gets the full value proposition.

**Read (2+ minutes):** Full paragraphs, feature explanations, FAQ. The evaluating buyer gets complete detail.

All three must work independently. A landing page that only works for full readers fails most visitors.

### The Conversion Belief Sequence

Visitors convert when they develop five beliefs in sequence:
1. This product solves my problem (relevance)
2. It actually works (credibility)
3. I can trust this company (trust)
4. The price is worth it (value)
5. I should act now (urgency)

Landing page copy must address all five in that order. Leading with trust signals (logos, awards) before establishing relevance is a common structural error: visitors who do not yet understand what the product does do not care about your awards.

## Output Format

```
LANDING PAGE COPY FRAMEWORK

Page: [product / campaign / event page]
Primary audience: [ICP]
Primary conversion action: [trial signup / demo request / download]

---

HERO SECTION
Headline: [Core value claim. Problem-led or outcome-led. Under 12 words.
Not the product name. Not "Welcome to X."]
Subhead: [1 to 2 sentences expanding on the headline. 20 to 30 words.]
Hero CTA: [Verb + specific outcome. "Start free trial" / "See it in action"]
Friction reducer: ["No credit card required" / "Free 14-day trial" / "Cancel anytime"]
Credibility hook: ["Trusted by 2,000+ teams" / "Rated 4.8/5 on G2"]

---

SOCIAL PROOF BAR
[Customer logos: 5 to 8 recognizable logos]
[Optional: "Join [N]+ companies" or award badge]

---

PROBLEM SECTION (for awareness-stage pages)
Section header: [the problem in 4 to 6 words]
Body: [2 to 3 short paragraphs or a before/after comparison]

---

VALUE PROPOSITION SECTIONS
For each core value (2 to 4 sections):
Section header: [outcome-led, 5 to 8 words]
Body: [2 to 3 sentences. Feature + benefit + proof. First sentence can stand alone for skimmers.]
Visual: [description of supporting screenshot or illustration]
Data point (optional): [specific metric supporting this claim]

---

SOCIAL PROOF SECTION
Primary quote: "[Specific outcome in customer's own words]"
[Full Name], [Title], [Company]
Supporting quotes (optional): [2 to 3 shorter quotes in a grid layout]

---

FEATURE/CAPABILITY SECTION (for evaluation-stage pages)
For each feature (3 to 6):
Feature label: [benefit-led label, not engineering name]
Description: [1 to 2 sentences. What it does and why it matters to this audience.]

---

OBJECTION HANDLING / FAQ SECTION
For each top objection or question:
Q: [How a skeptical visitor would phrase it]
A: [Direct, specific response. 2 to 3 sentences.]

---

FINAL CTA SECTION
Headline: [Reinforces primary value. Creates urgency without artificial pressure.]
Subhead: [Reduces friction. Addresses the hesitation that stopped them on the first CTA.]
CTA: [Same language as hero CTA for consistency]
Trust elements: [security badge / guarantee / trial terms]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketer writing landing page copy.

Page type: [product / campaign / event]
Primary audience: [specific ICP with role and company context]
Primary CTA: [the single conversion action]
Hero message: [the core claim in 10 words or fewer]
Value sections (2 to 4): [the main value areas to cover]
Proof available: [quotes, logos, and metrics to use]
Top objections to handle: [what stops visitors from converting]
Friction reducers: [trial terms, guarantees, security info]

Produce a complete landing page copy framework covering all sections.
Every headline must be outcome-led or problem-led, never feature-led.
Include at least 3 specific proof points with numbers.
Include at least 3 objection/FAQ responses.
```
