# one-pager.md

> **Skill:** Produces a product one-pager for use in sales conversations, prospect outreach, and digital deal rooms that communicates value, capabilities, and a clear next step on a single page.

---

## TLDR

A product one-pager gives a prospect the essential information about a product on a single page: the problem it solves, what it does, who trusts it, and what to do next. Designed to be left behind after a sales meeting, attached to an outbound email, or shared in a digital deal room. Every element earns its place because there is only one page.

---

## Topic Explanation

### One-Pager Design Principles

**Lead with the problem, not the product.** A prospect who recognizes their pain in the headline is immediately engaged. A prospect who reads a product name first must do cognitive work to connect it to their situation.

**One primary audience.** A one-pager speaking to both technical evaluators and economic buyers speaks to neither well. Different audiences get different one-pagers.

**One primary action.** Book a demo, start a free trial, or talk to sales. Not all three. Multiple CTAs dilute each other.

**Proof over claims.** "Customers reduce onboarding time by 65%" is more persuasive than "faster onboarding." Attach a number, a name, or a specific outcome to every key claim.

## Output Format

```
ONE-PAGER

HEADLINE
[Problem-led or outcome-led. 8 to 12 words.
Not the product name. Not "Introducing X."]

SUBHEAD
[One sentence of supporting context. 15 to 25 words.]

THE PROBLEM
[2 to 3 sentences. The pain the target reader faces today.
Written in second person: "You are spending..."]

THE SOLUTION
[2 to 3 sentences. What the product is and what it does.
Introduce the product name here for the first time.]

KEY CAPABILITIES (3)
[Label]: [Benefit statement] - [1 sentence explanation of what it does and why it matters]
[Label]: [Benefit statement] - [1 sentence explanation]
[Label]: [Benefit statement] - [1 sentence explanation]

PROOF
Option A (customer quote):
"[Quote about a specific outcome]"
[Full Name], [Title], [Company name]

Option B (metrics):
[Result 1] | [Result 2] | [Result 3]
(e.g., "65% faster onboarding" | "4.8/5 on G2" | "Trusted by 2,000+ teams")

Option C (logo bar):
[3 to 6 recognizable customer logos]

CALL TO ACTION
[Primary button: Verb + specific outcome]
[Supporting line reducing friction: "No credit card required" or "See a demo in 20 minutes"]

FOOTER
[Company name] | [Website] | [Contact info or sales email]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketer writing a product one-pager.

Product: [name and what it does]
Primary audience: [specific role and company type]
The problem: [the specific pain this audience experiences]
Key capabilities (3): [features framed as outcomes for this reader]
Proof: [customer quotes, metrics, or recognizable customer names]
Primary CTA: [the single action this document should drive]
Tone: [professional / technical / conversational]

Produce a complete one-pager.
The headline must lead with the problem or outcome, not the product name.
Every capability must be stated as a benefit to the reader, not a feature description.
The CTA must be specific: not "learn more" but a concrete action and outcome.
```
