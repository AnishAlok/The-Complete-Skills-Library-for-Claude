# sales-enablement-doc.md

> **Skill:** Produces a sales enablement document that trains the revenue team on a new product, feature, or market with the knowledge, messaging, and tools needed to sell confidently.

---

## TLDR

A sales enablement document equips sales representatives with everything needed to sell a new product or feature: what it is in plain language, who to target, what problem it solves, how to pitch it, what questions to ask, how to handle objections, and where to find supporting tools. Produced before a major launch or when a product significantly changes.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Sales Enablement Fails

Too product-centric: it describes what the product does in detail but does not translate features into sales narratives that resonate with buyers. Features are not a story. Outcomes are.

Too long: a 40-page document is not read by reps between calls. The most useful enablement is scannable, structured for quick reference, and focused on what reps need in the moment of a live conversation.

Not tested with reps: PMM writes the document, publishes it, and considers the job done. Effective enablement is developed with top-performing reps, tested in role-plays, and refined based on what actually works in deals.

### The SPIN Discovery Framework

SPIN (Rackham) provides a rigorous discovery structure for sales enablement:

Situation questions: establish context without interrogating. "How are you currently handling X?"
Problem questions: surface dissatisfaction. "What challenges does that create?"
Implication questions: expand the cost of the problem. "What impact does that have on your team's ability to Y?"
Need-payoff questions: create buy-in for the solution. "How valuable would it be if you could eliminate that step entirely?"

Including SPIN-structured discovery questions in enablement gives reps a repeatable conversation framework.

## Output Format

```
1. ENABLEMENT DOC HEADER
   Product/feature, date, PMM owner. Sales training session date/link.

2. THE ONE-PARAGRAPH PITCH
   [What this product does, for whom, and why they should care.
   Written as spoken language a rep can internalize and make their own.
   Not a script to recite. A mental model to adapt.]

3. WHO TO TARGET
   ICP: company-level firmographic characteristics.
   Best-fit titles to reach.
   Qualification criteria: signs of a good-fit deal.
   Disqualification criteria: signs this is not the right deal to pursue.

4. THE PROBLEM WE SOLVE
   [The specific pain in the buyer's own language.
   Functional dimension: what breaks down.
   Emotional dimension: how it affects them personally.]

5. HOW WE SOLVE IT
   Feature to benefit translation:
   | Feature | What it does | Why the buyer cares |

6. THE PITCH NARRATIVE
   Opening: [how to frame the first 2 minutes of a discovery call]
   Discovery: [the conversation arc that surfaces pain before pitching]
   Demo arc: [the narrative flow for a demo of this product]
   Recommended next step: [what a good close looks like]

7. DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (SPIN)
   Situation: [2 to 3 questions to establish context]
   Problem: [2 to 3 questions to surface dissatisfaction]
   Implication: [2 to 3 questions to expand the cost of the problem]
   Need-payoff: [2 to 3 questions to create buy-in for the solution]

8. OBJECTION HANDLING
   For each top objection:
   Objection: [exact language buyers use]
   Root concern: [what they actually mean]
   Response: [specific language to use]
   Proof: [supporting evidence or reference]

9. COMPETITIVE CONTEXT
   Key competitors likely to appear in these deals.
   Reference battlecards by name.
   Guidance on when and how to raise competition.

10. TOOLS AND RESOURCES
    Demo environment access: [link/instructions]
    Sales deck: [link]
    One-pager: [link]
    Battlecards: [links by competitor]
    Case studies: [links by industry/use case]
    ROI calculator: [link]
    Pricing and packaging: [link]
    Deal support escalation: [name and contact]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketing manager writing a sales enablement document.

Product/feature: [what sales needs to be enabled on]
Target buyer: [ICP firmographics and buying titles]
The problem: [specific pain in the buyer's language]
Key capabilities (3): [the most important things it does]
Common objections: [what buyers push back on]
Competitors in these deals: [who comes up most often]
Sales cycle context: [typical deal size, cycle length, key stakeholders]
Available tools: [what collateral already exists]

Produce a complete sales enablement document.
Every objection response must be specific language, not a general approach.
Discovery questions must follow the SPIN framework.
The one-paragraph pitch must be written as spoken language a rep can adapt.
```
