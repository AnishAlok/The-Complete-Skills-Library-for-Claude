# pitch-deck.md

> **Skill:** Produces a sales pitch deck narrative, slide structure, and presenter notes that guide a prospect from problem awareness to solution conviction through a logical story arc.

---

## TLDR

A sales pitch deck is a structured narrative designed to move a prospect from their current situation to conviction that this product is the right solution. This skill produces the slide structure, the content for each slide, and the presenter narrative notes for what to say while each slide is displayed. The deck is a presentation guide, not a leave-behind document.

---

## Topic Explanation

### The Deck Is Not the Presentation

A critical distinction: the slides provide visual anchors and proof points. The presenter delivers the narrative verbally. Decks with minimal text force presenters to know their material and create a more engaging conversation. Decks that are fully readable make the presenter redundant.

This skill produces both the slide content (what appears on screen) and the presenter narrative notes (what the presenter says while that slide is displayed).

### The Story Arc That Converts

The most effective B2B sales deck structure:

1. Their world: start with the prospect's situation. Demonstrate you understand their business before talking about yours.
2. The problem: why what they are doing today is insufficient. Create urgency without making them feel foolish.
3. A new way: introduce the insight that makes your solution the natural answer. Do not show the product yet.
4. Your solution: introduce the product as the embodiment of the new way.
5. Proof: customers who look like them achieving outcomes they want.
6. How it works: just enough technical credibility without overwhelming.
7. Why us: specific reasons to choose this company over alternatives.
8. Next step: one clear ask.

The most common failure: jumping from slide 1 directly to slide 4. Prospects disengage because they have not been made to feel understood first.

## Output Format

```
PITCH DECK STRUCTURE
Product: [name] | Audience: [role, company type] | Version: [X.X]

SLIDE 1: COVER
Visual: [clean, brand-consistent design with product logo and tagline]
Tagline: [should capture the problem or outcome, not the product name]
Presenter notes: Set the agenda verbally. "Today I want to understand your situation
before I show you anything. I'll spend the first few minutes learning about you,
then show you specifically how [product] addresses what I'm hearing."

SLIDE 2: THEIR WORLD
Headline: [Acknowledges the prospect's context without being condescending]
Content: [2 to 3 bullets describing the business context of a typical prospect in this segment]
Presenter notes: [Read the room. Validate each point with the prospect. "Is this familiar?"]

SLIDE 3: THE PROBLEM
Headline: [Problem statement creating tension]
Content: [Visual or data illustrating why the current approach fails. Not abstract.]
Presenter notes: [Surface the emotional dimension. "What does this mean for your team personally?"]

SLIDE 4: THE INSIGHT (the new way)
Headline: [The observation that changes the frame]
Content: [The trend, research, or logical argument that makes a new approach necessary]
Presenter notes: [This is the intellectual hook. The prospect should feel like they are learning something.]

SLIDE 5: INTRODUCING [PRODUCT]
Headline: [Product name plus one-line value statement]
Content: [Single strong visual. Brief positioning statement. No feature list.]
Presenter notes: [Connect explicitly back to slide 4's insight.]

SLIDE 6: KEY CAPABILITIES
Headline: [Outcome-led]
Content: [3 capabilities as outcome statements. Visual for each.]
Presenter notes: [Connect each capability back to a specific pain from slide 3.]

SLIDE 7: CUSTOMER PROOF
Headline: [Specific customer outcome as headline]
Content: [Logo, quote, and 1 to 2 specific metrics from a customer similar to this prospect]
Presenter notes: [Choose the customer reference closest in profile to this prospect's company.]

SLIDE 8: HOW IT WORKS
Headline: [Simple process or architecture summary]
Content: [3 to 4 step process or simple architecture diagram. No deep technical detail.]
Presenter notes: [Goal: establish credibility and trigger product questions for a demo.]

SLIDE 9: WHY [COMPANY]
Headline: [Specific reason to choose us]
Content: [3 differentiators with proof. Specific and verifiable, not "we are the best."]
Presenter notes: [These must withstand scrutiny. Do not claim what you cannot demonstrate.]

SLIDE 10: NEXT STEP
Headline: [Clear, specific ask]
Content: [One recommended next action. Simple timeline or next-step process.]
Presenter notes: [Ask for a specific commitment: "Can we schedule a 30-minute technical
demo for your engineering team next week?"]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a product marketer building a sales pitch deck.

Product: [name and what it does]
Target audience: [role, company type, typical deal characteristics]
Prospect's typical situation: [the business context before the product]
The problem: [specific pain in the prospect's language]
The insight: [the observation that makes your solution the right approach]
Key capabilities: [3 most important things the product does]
Customer proof: [names, quotes, or metrics to include]
Differentiators: [specific reasons to choose this over alternatives]
Desired next step: [what action the deck should drive]

Produce a complete 10-slide pitch deck with slide content and presenter narrative notes.
Every slide must connect back to the story arc established in slides 1 to 4.
Proof must be specific: real or realistic customer outcomes with numbers.
```
