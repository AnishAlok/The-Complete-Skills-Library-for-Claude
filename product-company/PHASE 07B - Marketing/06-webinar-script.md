# webinar-script.md

> **Skill:** Produces a webinar run sheet and presenter script including opening, agenda, presentation sections, Q&A facilitation, and close with CTA.

---

## TLDR

A webinar script and run sheet gives the presenter team everything needed to run a polished, on-time, high-value virtual event: the pre-event checklist, the opening sequence, the timed agenda, full presenter notes for each section, Q&A facilitation structure, and the close with a specific CTA. Webinars are among the highest-converting content formats for MOFU audiences when executed well.

---

## Topic Explanation

### What Makes a Webinar Worth Attending

Attendees invest 45 to 60 minutes of focused attention. That investment demands real value: a specific, actionable takeaway they could not have gotten from a blog post, a vendor product tour repackaged as a "webinar" is not that.

The highest-performing webinars share two characteristics: they teach something genuinely useful, and they are paced so attendees feel engaged the entire time. Engagement drops sharply after 30 minutes of uninterrupted slides. Polling questions, direct questions to attendees, and live demonstrations break up the passive viewing pattern.

### Webinar Structure

**Hook (first 5 minutes):** earn the next 45 minutes. Open with the specific problem the audience has, confirm what they will learn, and establish speaker credibility in one sentence.

**Content (25 to 35 minutes):** deliver the promised value. Three to four major points. No more. Each point should be a specific, actionable insight, not a slide of bullet points.

**Demo or proof (5 to 10 minutes):** show, do not just tell. A live demonstration or a specific before/after customer story.

**Q&A (10 to 15 minutes):** the most undervalued part of a webinar. Real questions from real prospects reveal objections, gaps in messaging, and buying intent.

**Close (2 to 3 minutes):** summarize the learning, give a specific next step, and end on time. Always end on time.

## Output Format

```
WEBINAR RUN SHEET AND SCRIPT

Title: [webinar title]
Date and time: [date, time, time zone]
Duration: [total length]
Presenters: [names, titles]
Platform: [Zoom / Teams / GoToWebinar / other]
Host/moderator: [name]

---

PRE-EVENT CHECKLIST (T-15 minutes)
[ ] Presenter audio and video tested
[ ] Slides loaded and screenshare tested
[ ] Polling questions loaded
[ ] Moderator has question queue ready
[ ] Recording enabled
[ ] Backup presenter contact confirmed

---

RUN SHEET (TIMED)
[00:00] Start recording. Hold music plays. Slide: "Starting shortly."
[00:05] Welcome and housekeeping (Host)
[00:08] Speaker intro (Host introduces presenter)
[00:10] Content begins (Presenter)
[00:35] Demo / proof section
[00:45] Q&A begins (Moderator facilitates)
[00:57] Close and CTA (Presenter)
[01:00] Hard end

---

FULL SCRIPT

OPENING (Host, 00:00 to 00:08)
"Welcome everyone, thanks for joining us today. We will get started in just a moment.
Quick housekeeping: this webinar is being recorded, the replay will be sent to all
registrants within 24 hours. Use the chat to submit questions throughout,
and we will save [X] minutes at the end for live Q&A."

SPEAKER INTRO (Host, 00:08 to 00:10)
"I am [name], [title] at [company]. Today I am joined by [presenter name],
[one-sentence credibility statement]. [Presenter name], over to you."

HOOK (Presenter, 00:10 to 00:15)
[Open with the specific problem the audience came here to solve.
Do not open with "So excited to be here." Open with the audience's pain.]
"[Specific problem statement]. Today I am going to show you [specific promised outcome].
By the end of this session, you will have [tangible takeaway]."

AGENDA REVEAL (Presenter, 00:15)
"Here is what we will cover: [three to four clear agenda items with time expectations]."

SECTION 1: [Title] (Presenter, 00:15 to 00:25)
[Full presenter notes. What to say, not just what slide to show.
Key insight: [the specific learning from this section]
Transition: "[Bridge sentence connecting to next section]"]

POLLING QUESTION (Host, 00:25)
"Quick question for the audience: [poll question]. [30 seconds for responses.]
Interesting - I can see [X]% of you said [answer]. [Presenter], what does that tell you?"

SECTION 2: [Title] (Presenter, 00:25 to 00:35)
[Full presenter notes]

DEMO / PROOF (Presenter, 00:35 to 00:45)
[What to demonstrate or which customer story to tell. Step by step.]

Q&A (Moderator, 00:45 to 00:57)
"We have [X] questions in the queue."
Seed questions if live queue is light:
- "[Pre-prepared question 1]"
- "[Pre-prepared question 2]"
Transition to close: "One more question, and then [presenter] will wrap up."

CLOSE AND CTA (Presenter, 00:57 to 01:00)
"To recap what we covered today: [three-sentence summary of key learnings].
[Specific CTA: the one next step attendees should take.]
[That CTA] is in the chat right now. [Name], back to you."

HOST CLOSE (Host)
"Thank you [presenter]. The recording and [any promised resource] will be in your inbox
within 24 hours. Thank you all for joining. Have a great [rest of day/week]."
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are producing a webinar for a marketing or sales team.

Webinar title: [title]
Duration: [45 / 60 / 90 minutes]
Audience: [who is attending and their stage of awareness]
Core promise: [the specific thing attendees will learn or be able to do after]
Content outline: [the 3 to 4 main points to cover]
Demo or proof element: [what will be shown or demonstrated]
CTA: [the specific next step to offer at the close]
Presenters: [names and titles]

Produce a complete webinar run sheet and full presenter script.
The hook must open with the audience's problem, not presenter introductions.
Every section must have full presenter notes, not just slide titles.
Include at least one polling question with facilitation guidance.
```
