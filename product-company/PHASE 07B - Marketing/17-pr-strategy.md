# pr-strategy.md

> **Skill:** Produces a PR strategy and media outreach plan defining news angles, target publications, pitch approach, and measurement framework for earned media.

---

## TLDR

A PR strategy defines how the company earns media coverage: which stories are worth pitching, which journalists and publications to target, what the pitch approach is for each story type, how to build and maintain media relationships, and how coverage is measured. Earned media is among the most credible forms of marketing, but it requires a genuine news angle and a journalist-first approach to pitching.

---

## Topic Explanation

### What Journalists Actually Want

PR strategies fail when they treat media outreach as another marketing channel: volume-based, message-first, and indifferent to whether the story is actually interesting.

Journalists want: a story that is genuinely newsworthy to their readers, data or evidence they cannot get elsewhere, access to real sources (executives, customers), and a pitch that is concise and respects their time.

Journalists do not want: press releases forwarded without context, pitches that require them to write a product feature, exclusive offers sent to 50 other journalists simultaneously, or follow-up emails that just say "circling back."

The single most effective PR principle: make the journalist's job easier. Have a story worth telling, have the assets ready (data, executive availability, customer references), and send the pitch to the right person at the right time.

### Story Types and News Value

Not all company news is newsworthy to media. Understanding what qualifies helps prioritize where to invest PR effort:

**High news value:** original research with surprising findings, significant funding with a compelling company narrative, executive hire that changes the strategic direction, major customer or partnership announcement with permission to name names.

**Medium news value:** product launch if genuinely innovative (not just new), industry trend commentary tied to a timely news hook, company milestone with an interesting "how we got here" angle.

**Low news value (for traditional media):** feature releases, award wins (unless prestigious), executive appointments below C-suite.

## Output Format

```
PR STRATEGY

Company: [name]
Strategy period: [e.g., Q1 to Q2, Year]
PR lead: [internal owner or agency]
Date: [date]

---

1. PR OBJECTIVES
   What earned media coverage must achieve for the business.
   Primary objective: [brand awareness / category credibility / recruiting / investor perception]
   Coverage targets: [number of placements, tier of publication, time period]
   Measurement: [impressions, Domain Authority of coverage, share of voice vs. competitors]

2. NEWS ANGLES (STORY INVENTORY)
   For each potential story:
   Story: [working headline]
   News value: [why this is interesting to a journalist's audience, not just ours]
   Ideal timing: [when to pitch]
   Target publications: [specific outlets and journalist names]
   Assets needed: [data, executive availability, customer reference, visuals]
   Status: [confirmed / in development / speculative]

3. TARGET MEDIA LIST
   Tier 1 (must-win): [publications that would most impact business objectives]
   Tier 2 (strong value): [second-tier but relevant publications]
   Tier 3 (opportunistic): [trade press, niche publications, podcast appearances]

   For each target outlet:
   Publication | Journalist name | Beat | Contact | Relationship status | Last coverage

4. PITCH APPROACH BY STORY TYPE
   For each story type in the inventory:
   Story type: [funding / product launch / research / executive commentary]
   Pitch format: [exclusive / embargo / open pitch]
   Pitch length: [3 paragraphs maximum for most pitches]
   Subject line approach: [what gets opened in this journalist's inbox]
   Timing: [day of week and time of day, specific to beat]
   Follow-up protocol: [one follow-up maximum, timing, format]

5. MEDIA RELATIONSHIP STRATEGY
   How to build relationships before needing coverage.
   Engagement outside pitching: [commenting on their work, sharing their coverage, connecting at events]
   Proactive briefing schedule: [quarterly executive briefings with key journalists]
   Rapid response strategy: [how to comment on breaking news to build journalist relationships]

6. SPOKESPERSON STRATEGY
   Primary spokesperson: [name, title, topics they cover]
   Secondary spokespeople: [subject matter experts for specific beats]
   Media training status: [trained / needs training]
   Message discipline: [how spokespeople stay on-brand in unscripted conversations]

7. MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING
   Metrics tracked: [placements, impressions, Domain Authority, share of voice, sentiment]
   Reporting cadence: [monthly]
   Tools: [Cision / Muck Rack / manual tracking]
   Attribution: [how PR influence on pipeline or recruiting is measured if at all]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a PR strategist writing a media relations strategy.

Company: [name and what it does]
PR objective: [what earned media must achieve for the business]
News inventory: [the stories available to pitch in the next 6 months]
Target media: [tier 1 publications and specific journalists if known]
Spokesperson: [who will speak to media]
Current media relationships: [existing relationships to build from]
Competitive PR landscape: [how much coverage competitors are getting and where]

Produce a complete PR strategy.
News angles must assess genuine news value, not just company importance.
Media list must include specific journalist names and beats, not just publication names.
Pitch approach must be journalist-first in framing.
```
