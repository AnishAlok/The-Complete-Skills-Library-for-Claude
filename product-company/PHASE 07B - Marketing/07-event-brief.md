# event-brief.md

> **Skill:** Produces an event planning brief and run of show for a company-hosted or company-sponsored marketing event.

---

## TLDR

An event brief defines the strategic intent, logistics, audience, content agenda, and run of show for a marketing event: from an intimate customer roundtable to a large hosted conference. It aligns all contributing teams before execution begins and serves as the operational reference document during the event.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Event Strategy: What the Event Must Accomplish

Events are expensive relative to digital marketing, both in money and attention. Before planning logistics, define what success looks like beyond attendance. The strongest event briefs define success in business terms: pipeline generated, customer relationships deepened, prospects who advance a stage, press mentions, net new contacts added to the database.

Events without a business-outcome definition tend to be measured only by attendance, which is an output not an outcome.

### Event Types and Their Strategic Roles

**Hosted conference or summit:** brand authority, category leadership, large-scale pipeline. High cost, high return if executed well. Requires 6 to 12 months of lead time.

**Customer roundtable or advisory board:** deep relationship building, customer retention, product insight. Small audience (10 to 20), intimate format, high conversion of at-risk accounts or expansion opportunities.

**Webinar (virtual event):** lower cost MOFU content that scales. See webinar-script.md.

**Sponsored conference presence (booth + sessions):** brand visibility and lead capture in an existing audience's context. ROI depends heavily on pre-event outreach and post-event follow-through.

**Hosted dinner or experience:** senior executive relationship building. Small audience, no presentation, relationship-first format.

### Run of Show

A run of show is the minute-by-minute operational schedule for the event day. It is the single reference document for all event staff. A good run of show eliminates the chaos of everyone asking what happens next. Every transition, every speaker change, every AV cue, every catering window is documented.

## Output Format

```
EVENT BRIEF

Event name: [name]
Event type: [conference / roundtable / dinner / sponsored / virtual]
Date and location: [date, venue, city]
Host or co-host: [company and any partners]
Event owner: [name] | Date of brief: [date]

---

1. STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES
   Primary goal: [the business outcome this event must achieve]
   Secondary goals: [2 to 3 additional desired outcomes]
   Success metrics: [specific, measurable targets]
   How this event fits the broader marketing strategy: [context]

2. TARGET AUDIENCE
   Primary: [who this event is designed for]
   Target attendance: [number of attendees and composition]
   Invite strategy: [how attendees will be sourced]
   VIP or special segment: [any specific accounts or contacts to prioritize]

3. BUDGET SUMMARY
   Total budget: [amount]
   Venue: | Catering: | AV/Production: | Speakers: |
   Marketing/promotion: | Staffing: | Contingency: |
   Budget owner and approval process: [name]

4. CONTENT AND AGENDA
   Event theme or message: [the single idea the event communicates]
   Agenda overview:
   [Time] | [Session title] | [Format] | [Speaker] | [Duration]
   Key speakers: [names, titles, topics, confirmation status]
   Audience participation format: [panels / workshops / roundtables / networking]

5. LOGISTICS
   Venue: [name, address, capacity, AV capabilities]
   Catering: [meals, dietary accommodations, timing]
   Registration: [platform, process, deadline]
   Travel and accommodation: [if applicable for speakers or attendees]
   On-site team: [roles and names]

6. MARKETING AND PROMOTION
   Announcement date: [when invitations go out]
   Channels: [email / LinkedIn / paid / partner / direct outreach]
   Registration deadline: [date]
   Confirmation and reminder cadence: [dates and messages]
   Post-event follow-up plan: [within 24 hours, within 1 week]

7. RUN OF SHOW
   [Time] | [Activity] | [Owner] | [Notes/Cues]
   [e.g., 7:30 AM | Venue setup complete | Event manager | AV test by 7:45]
   [e.g., 8:30 AM | Registration opens | Front desk staff | Badge printing ready]
   [e.g., 9:00 AM | Welcome remarks | CEO | Slides loaded, mic tested]
   [Continue through event close and breakdown]

8. POST-EVENT
   Lead/contact capture: [how contacts are collected and entered into CRM]
   Follow-up owner and timeline: [who sends what and when]
   Content repurposing: [recording, summary, photos to be produced]
   Debrief date: [when the team reviews what worked and what did not]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a marketing manager writing an event brief.

Event type: [conference / roundtable / dinner / sponsored]
Event purpose: [the strategic business goal this event serves]
Target audience: [who should attend and how many]
Date and location: [confirmed or proposed]
Budget: [total budget]
Agenda: [the content structure or sessions planned]
Key speakers: [names and topics if known]
Success metrics: [how you will measure whether this event achieved its goal]

Produce a complete event brief with all 8 sections including a detailed run of show.
Every run of show entry must have a time, activity, owner, and any relevant operational notes.
Success metrics must be specific and numeric.
```
