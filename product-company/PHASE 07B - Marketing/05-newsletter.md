# newsletter.md

> **Skill:** Produces a company newsletter structure and issue copy including subject line, editorial sections, and CTAs for a recurring subscriber communication.

---

## TLDR

A company newsletter is a recurring email communication to subscribers that delivers consistent value on a cadence. This skill produces both the newsletter format/template design and the copy for a specific issue: subject line, preheader, section content, and CTAs. A good newsletter builds a habitual reading relationship with an audience; a bad one trains subscribers to ignore it.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Newsletter Strategy: Why Most Fail

Most company newsletters fail because they serve the company, not the reader. They announce product updates the reader does not care about, share blog posts the reader could have found themselves, and arrive with the frequency of guilt rather than the rhythm of a valued relationship.

The newsletters with the highest open rates share one characteristic: readers have a specific reason to open every issue. That reason is usually one of: exclusive information they cannot get elsewhere, a consistent useful format they have come to rely on, or a voice they genuinely enjoy.

Before writing a newsletter, define what the reader gets from every issue that they cannot get anywhere else.

### Newsletter Types

**Curated newsletter:** the editor selects and annotates the best content from across the internet on a topic. Value is curation and editorial perspective. Example: Morning Brew.

**Original editorial newsletter:** the editor writes original content on a topic, typically thought leadership or analysis. Value is the writer's unique perspective.

**Company/product newsletter:** updates on the company, product, and industry relevant to customers and prospects. Value is staying informed. Higher tolerance for product mentions because the audience opted in specifically for company news.

**Hybrid:** most successful company newsletters combine original editorial content with product updates and curated resources.

### Cadence and Open Rates

B2B newsletter benchmarks: 35 to 45% open rate for highly segmented, high-value lists. 20 to 30% for broader lists. Under 20% signals the content-to-frequency ratio is off: either publishing too often for the value being delivered, or the content is not connecting with the audience.

## Output Format

```
NEWSLETTER

Publication name: [newsletter name]
Issue number and date: [Issue #XX | Month Day, Year]
Theme or focus of this issue: [the editorial thread connecting this issue]

---

SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS
A: [Direct value statement. Under 50 characters.]
B: [Curiosity-gap. Under 50 characters.]
C: [Question. Under 50 characters.]
Recommended: [A / B / C and why]

PREHEADER TEXT
[Complements the subject line. Adds information without repeating it. Under 90 characters.]

---

HEADER
Publication name + issue number.
[Optional: one-sentence issue theme]

---

OPENING (EDITORIAL NOTE)
[2 to 4 sentences from the editor. Personal, direct, sets the editorial theme for this issue.
This is where voice lives. Do not open with "In this issue you will find..."]

---

SECTION 1: [Section name matching editorial format]
[Content for this section. Length appropriate to section type.]
[If link: CTA text + URL]

---

SECTION 2: [Section name]
[Content]

---

SECTION 3: [Section name]
[Content]

---

[PRODUCT/COMPANY UPDATE (if included)]
[Brief. 2 to 4 sentences. What it is, why it matters to the reader. One link.]

---

[CURATED RESOURCE (if included)]
[Title + 2-sentence annotation explaining why it is worth the reader's time. Link.]

---

CLOSING
[1 to 2 sentences. Warm sign-off. Sets up next issue if there is a recurring theme.]
[Editor name/signature]

---

FOOTER
Unsubscribe | Manage preferences | [Company address]
[Optional: "Forward to a colleague" CTA]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a content marketer writing a company newsletter issue.

Newsletter name and positioning: [what this newsletter is and why subscribers signed up]
Target audience: [who receives this]
Issue theme: [the editorial focus of this specific issue]
Section format: [describe the recurring sections, e.g., "one original article, two curated links, one product update"]
Original content for this issue: [the main thing you are writing about]
Product or company update to include: [if any]
Curated links to include: [titles and URLs if any]
Tone: [reference brand-voice-guide.md or describe: professional / conversational / opinionated]

Produce a complete newsletter issue with 3 subject line options, all sections written in full,
and appropriate CTAs.
The opening must be editorial and voice-forward, not a table of contents.
```
