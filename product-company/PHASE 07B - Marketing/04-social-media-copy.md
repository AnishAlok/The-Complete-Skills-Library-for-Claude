# social-media-copy.md

> **Skill:** Produces social media post copy for LinkedIn, X (Twitter), and Instagram with platform-specific formatting, character limits, and engagement optimization.

---

## TLDR

Social media copy for a company must balance brand voice, platform norms, audience expectations, and a specific content goal in a very small number of words. This skill produces platform-native posts for LinkedIn, X, and Instagram: each version adapted for that platform's format, tone, and algorithmic preferences, not just the same text copy-pasted across platforms.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Platform Adaptation Matters

LinkedIn, X, and Instagram are different media. What works on one actively hurts performance on another.

LinkedIn is a professional context with higher tolerance for longer posts, thought leadership, and industry commentary. Carousels and long-form posts often outperform links. The audience expects substantive professional content.

X (Twitter) rewards concision, wit, and timeliness. Character limits enforce brevity. Threads work for longer ideas but must hook in the first tweet. The culture rewards directness and opinions.

Instagram is a visual-first platform. The caption supports the image; it does not replace it. Heavy use of line breaks and emojis is normal and expected. Stories and Reels have different copy conventions than feed posts.

Copy-pasting the same text across all three platforms signals low effort and typically underperforms on all of them.

### Algorithmic Considerations

All major platforms currently suppress or reduce distribution for posts that include external links (because the platform wants users to stay on-platform). Best practice: post the content without a link, put the link in the first comment, and note "link in first comment" in the post itself. This is true for LinkedIn and increasingly for X.

Engagement in the first 30 to 60 minutes strongly influences distribution. Posts that ask a genuine question or invite a specific response tend to drive early engagement. Engagement bait ("comment YES if you agree") is penalized by most algorithms.

## Output Format

```
SOCIAL POST SET

Topic/content being promoted: [what this post is about or linking to]
Goal: [awareness / engagement / traffic / lead generation]
Primary audience: [who should see and respond to this]

---

LINKEDIN

Format: [Single post / Carousel / Article / Poll]
Character target: [under 1,300 for no truncation; up to 3,000 for thought leadership posts]

Hook line (first 2 to 3 lines before "see more"):
[This is the only line most followers will see. Must compel the click to expand.
Use a surprising stat, a provocative claim, or a question that creates a gap.]

Body:
[Short paragraphs. Single-sentence lines are fine and often preferred.
Use line breaks liberally. One idea per paragraph.]

Close:
[Reinforce the key insight. Ask a genuine question to drive comments.]

CTA:
[Link in first comment / Download in bio / See full [resource] in comments]
Hashtags (3 to 5): [#relevant #professional #tags]

---

X (TWITTER)

Format: [Single tweet / Thread]
Character limit: 280 per tweet

Single tweet:
[Under 280 characters. Hook + point + optional CTA.
If including a link, it takes 23 characters regardless of actual length.]

Thread (if needed):
1/ [Hook tweet. Must stand alone and earn the thread read.]
2/ [First point]
3/ [Second point]
[Continue]
Last/ [Summary or CTA. "Thread starts above ^" is standard thread close.]

---

INSTAGRAM

Format: [Feed post / Reel caption / Story]
Caption approach: [Hook in first line before "more." Line breaks after every 1 to 2 sentences.]

Caption:
[Line 1: hook before "more" truncation]
[blank line]
[Body: short sentences with line breaks. Emojis acceptable and expected.]
[blank line]
[CTA: "Link in bio" / "Save this post" / "Tag someone who needs this"]

Hashtags (10 to 15): [#relevant #specific #broader #community tags]
[Post hashtags as first comment or at end of caption depending on brand preference]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a social media manager writing platform-specific posts.

Content topic: [what is being communicated or promoted]
Core message: [the main point in one sentence]
Target audience: [who should see and engage with this]
Goal: [awareness / engagement / traffic to a specific URL]
Platforms needed: [LinkedIn / X / Instagram / all three]
Tone: [professional / conversational / educational / bold]
Any must-include elements: [specific stat, quote, or CTA]

Produce platform-native copy for each requested platform.
Each platform version must be adapted for that platform's norms, not copy-pasted.
LinkedIn hook must compel the "see more" click.
X tweet must work in under 280 characters as a standalone.
Instagram caption must lead with a hook before the truncation point.
```
