# award-submission.md

> **Skill:** Produces a structured industry award submission matching the judges' evaluation criteria with compelling narrative, specific evidence, and quantified impact.

---

## TLDR

An award submission is a persuasive document that makes the case for why a company, product, team, or individual deserves recognition against a specific judging criteria. This skill produces the submission: a structured response to each evaluation criterion backed by specific evidence, written in a voice that is confident without being boastful and specific without being cluttered.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Most Award Submissions Fail

Award submissions fail for the same reason most persuasive writing fails: they make claims without evidence. "We have built an industry-leading product that delivers exceptional customer outcomes" is an assertion. "Our customers achieved an average 40% reduction in processing time within 90 days, and we retained 96% of our customer base last year" is evidence.

Judges evaluate dozens to hundreds of submissions. The ones that stand out are not the most polished; they are the most specific. Every superlative without a supporting number is a sentence that could be deleted without loss.

### Reading the Criteria

Every award has judging criteria. The submission must respond to those criteria explicitly, not implicitly. If the criteria lists "innovation, customer impact, and growth," the submission must address all three directly, ideally in the section or paragraph structure.

The most common submission mistake: answering the questions you wish had been asked instead of the questions that were actually asked.

## Output Format

```
AWARD SUBMISSION

Award name: [name of the award]
Category: [the specific category being entered]
Submitting company/team/individual: [name]
Submission owner: [name and contact]
Submission deadline: [date]

---

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (if required by the award)
[150 to 250 words. The complete case in miniature.
Who you are, what you did, and the specific impact you achieved.
Written to stand alone if the judge reads nothing else.]

---

RESPONSE TO EVALUATION CRITERIA

For each criterion (follow the award's exact structure):

CRITERION: [Exact criterion name from the award guidelines]
Word/page limit: [if specified]

Response:
[Open with the most compelling specific evidence for this criterion.
Do not open with company background unless the criterion asks for it.]

[Supporting evidence: 2 to 4 specific, quantified examples.
Every claim should have a number, a customer name, or a date.]

[If the criterion asks for innovation or a new approach: describe specifically
what existed before, what changed, and what the measurable result was.]

[If the criterion asks for customer or market impact: use specific customer outcomes,
not generic satisfaction statements.]

[Close each criterion response by explicitly connecting back to the criterion's language.]

---

SUPPORTING EVIDENCE (APPENDIX, if allowed)
Customer testimonials: [relevant quotes with full attribution]
Data visualizations: [if the submission format allows]
Media coverage: [relevant press if criterion includes visibility]
Team credentials: [if individual award, relevant background]

---

SUBMISSION CHECKLIST
[ ] Word count within limits for each section
[ ] All statistics cited with sources
[ ] Customer quotes approved for use
[ ] Submission formatted per guidelines
[ ] Supporting materials within page/file limits
[ ] Submitted before deadline
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are writing an industry award submission.

Award and category: [specific award and category being entered]
Evaluation criteria: [the exact criteria from the award guidelines]
Subject of the submission: [company / product / team / individual]
Key achievements to feature: [the strongest evidence of performance]
Customer evidence: [quotes or data that can be cited]
Growth or impact metrics: [specific numbers demonstrating results]
Innovation story: [what is new, different, or distinctive about the approach]
Word limits per section: [if specified by the award]

Produce a complete award submission following the evaluation criteria exactly.
Every criterion response must open with specific evidence, not company background.
Every claim must be supported by a specific number, customer name, or verifiable result.
```
