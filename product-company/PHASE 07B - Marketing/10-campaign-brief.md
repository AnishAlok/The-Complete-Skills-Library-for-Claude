# campaign-brief.md

> **Skill:** Produces a campaign brief documenting the objective, audience, core message, channel plan, asset requirements, timeline, and KPIs for any marketing campaign.

---

## TLDR

A campaign brief is the single source of truth for a marketing campaign: it documents every strategic and operational decision so that every contributor — copywriter, designer, media buyer, analytics — is working from the same agreed foundation. A brief written before creative work begins prevents the expensive cycle of off-strategy creative that gets revised into mediocrity.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Campaigns Fail Without a Brief

The most common source of campaign failure is not poor execution. It is executing the wrong thing well. Campaigns fail when:

The objective is vague. "Increase brand awareness" is not an objective. "Generate 500 MQLs from VP of Engineering at 200 to 1000 person SaaS companies within 8 weeks, at a CPL under $150" is an objective. The difference determines every downstream decision.

The audience is too broad. Campaigns trying to speak to everyone speak to no one. Every audience expansion requires a message trade-off.

The core message is unclear. If the campaign team cannot agree on one sentence describing what the campaign communicates, the campaign will communicate multiple things weakly instead of one thing strongly.

The brief is written after the creative. This happens more than anyone admits. When the brief is an afterthought used to justify creative that already exists, it cannot serve its function.

### What a Brief Is Not

A campaign brief is not a creative brief. A creative brief is given to a designer or writer and focuses on executional details: dimensions, visual direction, tone, examples. A campaign brief is strategic: it is the document that authorizes and aligns the campaign before creative briefing begins.

## Output Format

```
CAMPAIGN BRIEF

Campaign name: [name]
Brief owner: [marketing lead] | Date: [date]
Campaign period: [start to end date]
Budget: [total approved budget]
Stakeholder approval required: [names]

---

1. CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE
   Business goal this campaign serves: [specific business outcome]
   Primary campaign KPI: [the one number that defines success]
   Target: [specific numeric goal with timeframe]
   Secondary KPIs: [2 to 3 supporting metrics]

2. TARGET AUDIENCE
   Segment: [specific ICP definition with firmographics and role]
   Stage of awareness: [unaware / problem aware / solution aware / product aware]
   Size of reachable universe: [estimated]
   Key insight about this audience: [the specific tension or truth that makes this campaign relevant to them right now]

3. CORE MESSAGE
   One-sentence campaign message: [the single thing this campaign communicates]
   Why this message for this audience at this time: [the strategic rationale]
   Message hierarchy: [primary message + 2 to 3 supporting messages]
   What this campaign is NOT saying: [scope of message, explicitly]

4. CAMPAIGN OFFER OR HOOK
   What is being offered or what creates the hook: [content / event / promotion / insight]
   Value to the audience: [why they should care]

5. CHANNEL PLAN
   Channel | Role in campaign | Audience targeting | Budget | KPI | Owner
   [e.g., LinkedIn paid | Awareness + traffic | VP Eng at 200-1000 person SaaS | $X | CTR, CPL | Media buyer]

6. ASSET REQUIREMENTS
   Asset | Channel | Format specs | Copy owner | Design owner | Due date
   [List every creative asset needed]

7. TIMELINE
   Brief approved: [date]
   Creative briefing: [date]
   Assets due: [date]
   Campaign launch: [date]
   Mid-campaign review: [date]
   Campaign end: [date]
   Final report: [date]

8. MEASUREMENT AND REPORTING
   Attribution model: [first touch / last touch / multi-touch]
   Reporting cadence: [weekly / bi-weekly]
   Reporting owner: [name]
   Dashboard or reporting tool: [where results will be tracked]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a marketing manager writing a campaign brief.

Campaign objective: [the specific business goal and numeric target]
Target audience: [precise ICP definition]
Core message: [the one thing this campaign communicates]
Campaign offer or hook: [what creates the value exchange]
Channels being used: [list of channels]
Budget: [total and channel allocation if known]
Timeline: [campaign start and end dates]
Team: [who is responsible for what]

Produce a complete campaign brief.
The objective must include a specific, numeric KPI with a timeframe.
The core message must be one sentence. It must be arguable and specific, not generic.
The asset requirements section must list every individual creative piece needed.
```
