# blog-post.md

> **Skill:** Produces a well-structured blog post with SEO-informed hierarchy, a compelling opening, scannable body, and a clear conclusion with CTA.

---

## TLDR

A blog post is a piece of written content published on the company blog to attract organic traffic, build topical authority, and serve the reader's informational need. This skill produces a complete blog post from title through CTA: SEO-informed structure, a hook that earns the read, a body that delivers on the title's promise, and a conclusion that converts interest into action.

---

## Topic Explanation

### The Blog Post Problem

Most company blog posts fail for one of three reasons:

They are written for the company, not the reader. "We are thrilled to announce..." and "Our product helps companies..." are company-first. Readers search because they have a question or problem; the post must be written from their perspective.

They rank without delivering. They use the keyword in the title and H1 but do not actually answer the question the reader came to have answered. Google has learned to detect this; so have readers.

They have no conversion path. The post gets traffic but asks for nothing and links to nothing relevant. A blog post that does not guide the reader to a next step is a missed opportunity at every stage of the funnel.

### SEO Fundamentals in Blog Posts

Title tag: the most important on-page SEO element. Must include the primary keyword. 50 to 60 characters. Matches search intent.

H1: the visible headline on the page. Usually matches the title tag. Should include the primary keyword.

H2s: the main section headers. Include secondary keywords naturally. Help readers scan and help search engines understand content structure.

Intro paragraph: the first 100 words are heavily weighted by search engines and determine whether readers stay. Answer the search intent immediately, then earn the deeper read.

Internal links: link to other relevant content on the site. Distributes page authority and keeps readers engaged.

---

## Dos

- **Do answer the search intent in the first paragraph.** If someone searches "how to write a job description," answer that question immediately in paragraph one. Then build depth.
- **Do write for skimmers.** Use H2s, H3s, bullet points, and bold text to allow readers to extract value without reading every word.
- **Do include one specific CTA.** Not multiple competing asks. One next step that is directly relevant to what the reader just read.

---

## Donts

- **Dont bury the lead.** Every word before the answer is a reason for the reader to leave.
- **Dont stuff keywords.** Write for humans. If a keyword appears naturally 3 to 4 times in a 1,000-word post, that is appropriate. Forcing it into every paragraph hurts readability and search performance.

## Output Format

```
BLOG POST

Title: [Primary keyword + value promise. 50 to 60 characters.]
Meta description: [150 to 160 characters. Expands on title. Includes keyword. Drives click.]
Estimated word count: [800 / 1200 / 1800 / 2500]
Target keyword: [primary keyword]
Secondary keywords: [2 to 4 related terms]
Funnel stage: [TOFU / MOFU / BOFU]

---

H1: [Matches or closely follows title. Includes primary keyword.]

INTRO (100 to 150 words)
[Hook: the first sentence must earn the rest. Use a surprising stat, a provocative claim,
or directly acknowledge the reader's question.]
[Answer in brief: address the search intent immediately. Do not save the answer for later.]
[Preview: tell the reader what they will get from reading to the end.]

H2: [First main section. Includes a secondary keyword naturally.]
[Body: 200 to 400 words. Delivers on the H2 promise. One idea per paragraph.
Short paragraphs: 2 to 4 sentences maximum.]

H2: [Second main section]
[Body]

H2: [Third main section]
[Body]

[Continue for additional sections as needed]

H2: Conclusion (or a titled summary)
[Restate the main takeaway in 2 to 3 sentences.
Do not introduce new information. Confirm the reader's learning.]

CTA
[One specific next step directly relevant to this post's topic.
Link to the most relevant product page, tool, or related piece of content.
Frame as a benefit: not "read our whitepaper" but "see how [specific thing] works."]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a content marketer writing a blog post.

Target keyword: [primary keyword with search intent]
Secondary keywords: [2 to 4 related terms]
Search intent: [what the reader wants to learn or accomplish]
Target audience: [role and stage of awareness]
Funnel stage: [TOFU / MOFU / BOFU]
Angle: [the specific perspective or unique take for this post]
Key points to cover: [the main ideas the post must address]
Word count target: [800 / 1200 / 1800]
CTA: [what action should the reader take after reading]

Write a complete blog post with title, meta description, H1, intro, all sections,
conclusion, and CTA.
Answer the search intent in the first paragraph.
Every H2 must deliver on its promise.
```
