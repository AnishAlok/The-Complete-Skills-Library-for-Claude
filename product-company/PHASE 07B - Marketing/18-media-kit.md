# media-kit.md

> **Skill:** Produces a press and media kit containing company overview, executive bios, product descriptions, key statistics, boilerplate copy, and press contact information.

---

## TLDR

A media kit is the collection of assets and information a journalist, analyst, podcast host, or event organizer needs to write accurately about the company. It is not a marketing document; it is a reference and source document. A media kit that is marketing-heavy and fact-light fails to serve its purpose because journalists cannot use brand claims in their coverage: they need facts, numbers, context, and verified quotes.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Who Uses a Media Kit and What They Need

**Journalists:** the company overview, key statistics, executive availability for quotes, and recent news. They want facts they can verify and spokespeople they can reach.

**Podcast and event hosts:** executive bios, suggested interview questions, headshots, and talking points. They want to look prepared without doing extensive research.

**Analysts:** company positioning, market claims with backing data, and competitive context. They want credibility signals and evidence.

**Partners and investors:** company overview, traction data, and leadership team. They want a sense of the company's narrative and credibility.

A media kit that only contains press releases and marketing copy serves none of these audiences well.

### What to Include (and What to Leave Out)

Include: verified facts, attributed statistics, executive bios with credibility-relevant detail, accurate product descriptions, approved photography and brand assets, and contact information for the person who responds to media.

Leave out: unsubstantiated superlatives ("industry-leading," "revolutionary"), claims that require a journalist to trust without evidence, and marketing copy that reads as brand promotion rather than information.

## Output Format

```
MEDIA KIT

Company: [name]
Version: [X.X] | Last updated: [date]
Media contact: [name, title, email, phone]

---

1. COMPANY OVERVIEW (300 to 400 words)
   What the company does (plain language, no jargon)
   Who it serves (specific customer description)
   How it is different from alternatives (fact-based differentiation)
   Current stage (founding year, funding, employee count, customer scale)
   Where it operates (HQ, additional offices, geographic coverage)

2. COMPANY BOILERPLATE (three versions)
   Short (50 words): [for tight credit lines and brief mentions]
   Medium (100 words): [for standard press release and article attribution]
   Long (200 words): [for in-depth coverage and backgrounders]

3. KEY FACTS AND STATISTICS
   [Verified, specific statistics that can be attributed to the company]
   Founded: [year]
   Headquarters: [city, state/country]
   Employees: [count or range]
   Customers: [count or notable names if approved]
   Funding: [total raised and stage, or public company status]
   [Product-specific traction: relevant volume metrics if shareable]
   [Any industry recognition or major partnerships]

4. EXECUTIVE BIOGRAPHIES
   For each spokesperson:
   [Full name], [Title]
   [100 to 150 word biography: current role, prior experience relevant to the company's story,
   educational credentials if relevant, one humanizing personal detail.
   Written in third person.]
   Headshot: [link to high-resolution download]
   LinkedIn: [URL]
   Available for: [media interviews / podcast appearances / event speaking / all]
   Topics: [the 3 to 5 topics this executive can speak to credibly]

5. PRODUCT/SERVICE DESCRIPTIONS
   For each key product or service:
   [Product name]: [2 to 3 sentence description. What it does. Who it is for. What makes it different.]
   Category: [what market category it belongs to]
   Pricing model: [if public]
   Availability: [where and how to access]

6. RECENT NEWS AND MILESTONES
   [Reverse chronological list of press releases, significant announcements, and media coverage]
   [Date] | [Headline] | [Publication if coverage] | [Link]

7. BRAND ASSETS
   Logo files: [link to download: full color, white, black, SVG, PNG, EPS]
   Executive headshots: [link to download: print resolution]
   Product screenshots: [link to download]
   Usage guidelines: [link to brand-voice-guide.md or separate brand guidelines]
   Do not edit: [specific usage restrictions]

8. PRESS CONTACT
   [Name]
   [Title]
   [Email - monitored and responsive within 24 hours for press inquiries]
   [Phone - for urgent inquiries]
   [Agency contact if applicable]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a marketing manager assembling a company media kit.

Company: [name and what it does]
Key facts: [founding year, funding, employee count, customer count or notable names]
Executive spokespeople: [names, titles, topics they cover]
Products or services: [names and descriptions]
Recent significant news: [last 6 to 12 months of announcements]
Brand assets available: [what file types exist]
Media contact: [who journalists should reach]

Produce a complete media kit.
All statistics must be specific and verifiable, not estimated ranges unless noted.
Executive bios must be written in third person, 100 to 150 words.
Boilerplate must be produced in three lengths.
```
