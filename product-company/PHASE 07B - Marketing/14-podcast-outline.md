# podcast-outline.md

> **Skill:** Produces a podcast episode outline with segment breakdown, host talking points, guest interview questions, and show notes for publishing.

---

## TLDR

A podcast episode outline gives the host the structure, talking points, interview questions, and segment timing needed to record a focused, valuable episode. This skill also produces the post-production show notes: the episode description, timestamps, key takeaways, and resource links published alongside the episode.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Why Podcast Outlines Matter

Unscripted podcasts are fine for conversational chemistry. Unstructured podcasts are not. The best podcasts feel spontaneous but are organized: the host knows where the conversation starts, where the major segments go, and where it ends. The guest is surprised by nothing and the listener gets a coherent arc.

An outline is not a script. A good interview still follows the guest's natural responses. The outline defines the frame; the conversation fills it.

### Show Notes as SEO and Content Repurposing

Show notes are underutilized by most company podcasts. Well-written show notes with timestamps, key quotes, and resource links do three things: help listeners navigate the episode, provide SEO-indexed text content, and create a repurposable asset for social and newsletter distribution.

The show notes for a 45-minute interview episode should be 300 to 600 words, include 5 to 8 timestamps, 3 to 5 key quotes or insights, and all resources mentioned.

## Output Format

```
PODCAST EPISODE OUTLINE

Podcast name: [name]
Episode number: [#XX]
Episode title: [working title]
Guest: [name, title, company] (or "Solo" if no guest)
Recording date: [date] | Publish date: [date]
Target length: [20 / 30 / 45 / 60 minutes]
Episode goal: [what listeners will learn or be able to do]

---

EPISODE STRUCTURE

INTRO ([X] minutes)
Host: [Name/s]
Hook: [The one compelling reason to listen to this episode - say this in the first 30 seconds]
Guest intro: [How to introduce the guest: their credibility in 2 sentences, not a full bio]
Episode promise: [Tell listeners exactly what they will get from listening to the end]

SEGMENT 1: BACKGROUND / CONTEXT ([X] minutes)
Purpose: [Establish the guest's relevant experience / set the context for today's topic]
Talking points:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
Key question: "[Opening question to establish credibility and ease the guest in]"
Follow-up questions:
- "[Follow-up 1]"
- "[Follow-up 2]"

SEGMENT 2: CORE TOPIC / MAIN CONTENT ([X] minutes)
Purpose: [The substance of the episode - the insight, framework, or story]
Talking points:
- [Point 1 with host context]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]
Key questions:
- "[The most important question of the episode]"
- "[Question that invites a specific example or story]"
- "[Question that surfaces a counterintuitive or surprising insight]"

SEGMENT 3: PRACTICAL APPLICATION ([X] minutes)
Purpose: [Actionable takeaways for the listener]
Talking points:
- [What listeners can do with what they just heard]
Key questions:
- "[What would you tell someone trying to implement this tomorrow?]"
- "[What do most people get wrong about X?]"

RAPID FIRE OR CLOSE SEGMENT ([X] minutes) (optional)
Format: [Quick questions / predictions / recommendations]
Questions:
- "[Question 1]"
- "[Question 2]"
- "[Question 3]"

OUTRO ([X] minutes)
Key takeaway summary: [Host synthesizes the 3 main insights in 3 sentences]
Guest CTA: [Where to follow the guest, their work, or a resource they mentioned]
Show CTA: [Subscribe / leave a review / newsletter signup]

---

SHOW NOTES

Episode title: [SEO-optimized title]
Episode description (150 to 200 words):
[Opens with the episode's core question or insight. Names the guest with one-line context.
Summarizes the 3 to 4 main topics covered. Ends with a reason to listen.]

Timestamps:
[00:00] Introduction and guest background
[XX:XX] [Segment topic]
[XX:XX] [Segment topic]
[XX:XX] [Key insight or story]
[XX:XX] Rapid fire / close

Key quotes:
"[Verbatim quote 1 from episode]" - [Guest name]
"[Verbatim quote 2]" - [Guest name]
"[Verbatim quote 3]" - [Guest name]

Resources mentioned:
[Book / article / tool name]: [link]

Connect with [Guest name]:
LinkedIn: [URL] | Website: [URL] | Twitter: [handle]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a podcast producer writing an episode outline.

Podcast name and format: [what kind of show this is]
Episode topic: [what this episode is about]
Guest: [name, title, and why they are the right guest for this topic]
Core insight or question: [the main thing listeners will get from this episode]
Episode length: [target runtime]
Segments needed: [how many segments and at what depth]
Show notes requirements: [SEO focus keyword if applicable]

Produce a complete episode outline with all segments, questions, and talking points,
plus publish-ready show notes with timestamps, quotes, and resource list.
The hook must be specific to this episode, not generic podcast introduction language.
Every interview question must be a genuine question, not a prompt for a monologue.
```
