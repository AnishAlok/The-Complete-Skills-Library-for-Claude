# affiliate-program-doc.md

> **Skill:** Produces affiliate program documentation covering program structure, commission model, partner criteria, onboarding, creative assets, and performance expectations.

---

## TLDR

An affiliate program document defines how the company's affiliate program works: who qualifies, what they earn, how they are tracked, what assets they receive, and what performance is expected. This documentation serves two purposes: it is the operational reference for the internal team running the program, and it is the onboarding document shared with prospective and newly approved affiliates.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Affiliate vs. Referral vs. Partner Program

These terms overlap in practice but have distinct meanings:

**Affiliate program:** typically performance-based, open to a broad range of publishers and content creators who promote the product in exchange for a commission on referred sales or leads. Scale is the goal.

**Referral program:** typically for existing customers who refer friends or colleagues. Simpler structure, smaller incentive (discount, credit, or small cash reward). Trust is the mechanism.

**Partner program:** typically for businesses (agencies, consultants, resellers) who bring the product to their clients. Higher-touch, higher-commission, with formal relationship management.

This skill covers affiliate programs. For partner programs, a separate template is warranted.

### Commission Model Design

The commission model determines both who joins the program and how hard they promote:

**Percentage of sale:** affiliate earns X% of every purchase they refer. Scales with deal size. Simple to understand.

**Flat fee per conversion:** affiliate earns $X for every qualified lead or customer they refer. Predictable cost per acquisition. Works well when average deal size varies widely.

**Tiered commission:** higher commissions for affiliates who generate above a threshold volume. Incentivizes top performers to prioritize the program.

**Cookie window:** how long after a click the affiliate receives credit for a conversion. 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day windows are standard. Longer windows favor affiliates in content with long consideration cycles.

## Output Format

```
AFFILIATE PROGRAM DOCUMENTATION

Company: [name]
Program name: [e.g., [Company] Partner Affiliate Program]
Version: [X.X] | Date: [date] | Program owner: [name]

---

1. PROGRAM OVERVIEW
   What the program is and who it is for.
   The value proposition for affiliates: why promoting this product is worth their time.
   Program tier structure (if applicable): [standard / preferred / elite]

2. AFFILIATE ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
   Who can apply: [content creators, bloggers, review sites, community managers, etc.]
   Minimum requirements: [audience size, traffic, content quality, topic alignment]
   Disqualifying factors: [competitor affiliation, content type violations, geographic restrictions]
   Approval process: [auto-approve vs. manual review, timeline]

3. COMMISSION STRUCTURE
   Commission type: [percentage / flat fee / tiered]
   Commission rates: [by tier or product if applicable]
   Cookie window: [days]
   Attribution model: [last click / first click / linear]
   Minimum payout threshold: [amount before payment is issued]
   Payment schedule: [monthly / net 30 / net 60]
   Payment method: [bank transfer / PayPal / Stripe]

4. WHAT AFFILIATES PROMOTE
   Products or plans eligible for commission.
   Products or plans excluded from the program.
   Promotable offers: [free trial, discount codes, lead magnets]
   Current offers available: [specific promotional codes or links]

5. TRACKING AND REPORTING
   Tracking platform: [Impact / ShareASale / PartnerStack / in-house]
   How to access the affiliate dashboard.
   UTM and tracking link structure.
   What metrics are visible: [clicks, conversions, commissions, pending payouts]
   Reporting cadence: [real-time / daily / weekly]

6. CREATIVE ASSETS
   What is provided: [banners, logos, product screenshots, copy, social posts]
   Where to access assets: [link to asset library]
   Brand usage guidelines: [what affiliates can and cannot do with brand assets]
   Custom asset requests: [process and timeline]

7. CONTENT AND PROMOTION GUIDELINES
   Approved promotion methods: [content, email, social, PPC (if allowed), review sites]
   Prohibited methods: [trademark bidding, coupon sites if excluded, misleading claims]
   Disclosure requirements: [FTC/ASA affiliate disclosure language required]
   Claims affiliates can and cannot make: [approved claims / prohibited claims]

8. ONBOARDING PROCESS
   Step 1: Application [link]
   Step 2: Approval [timeline]
   Step 3: Account setup in tracking platform
   Step 4: Access assets and generate tracking links
   Step 5: First content review (optional for new affiliates)
   Support contact: [email and response time expectation]

9. PERFORMANCE EXPECTATIONS AND PROGRAM BENEFITS
   Activity requirements to remain active: [minimum clicks or conversions per period]
   Tier advancement criteria: [what unlocks preferred or elite status]
   Benefits by tier: [higher commission, dedicated support, exclusive offers]
   Program terms and termination: [reference to full terms and conditions]
```
