# influencer-brief.md

> **Skill:** Produces an influencer campaign brief covering program objectives, creator criteria, content guidelines, deliverables, compensation, and usage rights.

---

## TLDR

An influencer brief is the document given to a content creator or influencer when partnering on a campaign. It defines the campaign objective, the audience the brand wants to reach, the content direction without over-scripting, the deliverables expected, the timeline, the compensation, and the usage rights. A good brief gives the creator enough guidance to stay on-brand while leaving enough creative latitude to stay authentic to their audience.

---

## Topic Explanation

### The Brief Paradox: Direction vs. Authenticity

The most common influencer brief failure: over-scripting. When a brand provides exact language for a creator to read verbatim, the creator's audience immediately recognizes an advertisement, engagement drops, and the campaign underperforms.

The brief must provide direction without removing authenticity. Direction means: the key message to communicate, the product feature to demonstrate, the claim that must be included for legal compliance, and what not to say. It does not mean: a script to read, a tone that is not the creator's voice, or a narrative that the creator would never naturally tell.

The most effective influencer content is "creator-first, brand-informed": the creator tells a story in their natural voice, and the product fits naturally into that story.

### B2B vs. B2C Influencer Strategy

B2C influencer marketing is dominated by follower counts and aesthetic fit. B2B influencer marketing (often called "creator" or "thought leader" marketing on LinkedIn) is dominated by audience quality and topical authority.

A LinkedIn creator with 20,000 highly engaged followers in a specific industry vertical is worth more to a B2B brand than a LinkedIn creator with 200,000 followers in a general business audience.

## Output Format

```
INFLUENCER CAMPAIGN BRIEF

Campaign name: [name]
Brand: [company name]
Campaign period: [start to end]
Brief owner: [name and contact]

---

1. CAMPAIGN OVERVIEW
   What this campaign is: [1 to 2 sentences on the campaign context]
   Why we are working with creators: [the strategic role of influencer in this campaign]
   Target audience for this campaign: [the specific audience we want to reach through the creator]

2. CREATOR CRITERIA
   Audience demographics: [platform, follower count range, audience industry/role]
   Topical alignment: [topics the creator covers that align with the campaign]
   Engagement quality: [minimum engagement rate, comment quality expectations]
   Brand safety: [topics, associations, or content types that disqualify a creator]
   Example creators who fit (if identified): [names if applicable]

3. CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE AND KPIs
   Primary objective: [awareness / engagement / traffic / leads]
   Primary KPI: [reach / impressions / clicks / conversions]
   Tracking method: [UTM links / discount codes / pixel / survey]

4. KEY MESSAGE (what to communicate)
   The core message: [1 sentence the creator should leave the audience with]
   Must-include information: [product name, specific feature, offer, or claim]
   Proof points the creator can draw on: [data, results, or stories to reference]

5. CONTENT GUIDELINES
   Do include: [specific elements, themes, or angles that work]
   Do not include: [off-limits topics, competitor mentions, unverified claims]
   Disclosure requirement: [#ad / #sponsored / #partner as required by FTC/ASA]
   Brand asset access: [logos, product images, trial access if applicable]

6. DELIVERABLES
   For each deliverable:
   Platform | Format | Quantity | Length/specs | Required elements | Due date

   Example:
   LinkedIn | Long-form post | 1 | 500 to 1000 words | Must include [X] and UTM link | [date]
   LinkedIn | Story/carousel | 1 | 5 to 8 slides | Must tag brand account | [date]

7. APPROVAL PROCESS
   Draft submission deadline: [date before go-live]
   Review turnaround: [how quickly the brand will respond]
   Number of revision rounds: [typically 1 to 2]
   Approval contact: [name and email]

8. COMPENSATION AND USAGE RIGHTS
   Compensation: [flat fee / per post / performance-based / product only]
   Payment terms: [net 30 / upon delivery / split: on signing and on completion]
   Usage rights: [organic only / paid amplification / duration / platforms]
   Exclusivity: [category exclusivity period if applicable]
   Contract reference: [link to agreement template]
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a marketing manager writing an influencer campaign brief.

Campaign context: [what the broader campaign is about]
Target audience: [who you want to reach through influencers]
Creator type: [platform, audience size, topical focus]
Key message: [the one thing the creator must communicate]
Deliverables: [what content pieces are expected]
Timeline: [campaign period and content due dates]
Compensation approach: [flat fee / performance / product]
Usage rights needed: [how the brand plans to use the content]

Produce a complete influencer brief.
Content guidelines must distinguish between direction and scripting.
Disclosure requirements must comply with FTC/ASA guidance.
Deliverables must include all format specs and due dates.
```
