# content-strategy.md

> **Skill:** Produces a content strategy document defining content pillars, audience mapping, format mix, distribution channels, editorial calendar structure, and measurement framework.

---

## TLDR

A content strategy defines how a company will use content to achieve business objectives: which topics to own, which formats to use, which audiences to serve at each stage of the funnel, how often to publish, and how to measure whether it is working. Without a strategy, content teams produce output without impact. With one, every piece of content connects back to an audience need and a business goal.

---

## Topic Explanation

### Content Strategy vs. Content Calendar

Content strategy: the decisions about what to create, for whom, why, and how to measure it. Strategic, relatively stable for 6 to 12 months.

Content calendar: the execution schedule of specific pieces. Tactical, updated weekly or monthly.

A content calendar without a strategy is a schedule of activity with no coherent purpose. The strategy comes first; the calendar implements it.

### Content Pillars

A content pillar is a broad topic area the brand commits to owning deeply. Pillar topics should sit at the intersection of three criteria:

1. Audience care: the target audience genuinely needs this information
2. Brand authority: the company has real expertise or a distinctive perspective to contribute
3. Business relevance: ranking for or being known for this topic drives pipeline or retention

Three to five pillars are usually right. More than five dilutes focus. Fewer than three limits SEO surface area and audience reach.

### The Content Funnel

Content must serve buyers at different stages of awareness:

**Top of funnel (TOFU):** awareness and education. Audiences do not yet know the product but have the problem. Blog posts, thought leadership, social content, podcasts.

**Middle of funnel (MOFU):** consideration and evaluation. Audiences know the product category and are comparing options. Webinars, comparison guides, case studies, whitepapers.

**Bottom of funnel (BOFU):** decision and conversion. Audiences are ready to buy and need final validation. Case studies, demos, ROI calculators, proposal content.

A healthy content strategy produces content at all three stages. Most companies over-index on TOFU and neglect MOFU.

## Output Format

```
1. STRATEGY HEADER
   Company, audience, date, content lead.
   Review cadence: quarterly strategy review, monthly calendar review.

2. BUSINESS OBJECTIVES
   What business goals this content strategy serves.
   How content contributes to pipeline, retention, or brand.
   Primary content metric that maps to business outcome.

3. TARGET AUDIENCE
   Primary audience: role, company type, stage of awareness.
   Secondary audiences: ranked by priority.
   For each audience: what they need, what they search for, where they consume content.

4. CONTENT PILLARS
   For each pillar (3 to 5):
   Pillar name: [broad topic area]
   Why we own this: [our unique perspective or expertise]
   Audience relevance: [why the target audience cares]
   Business relevance: [how ranking for or being known for this drives business outcomes]
   Sub-topics: [5 to 10 specific topics within this pillar]
   Formats best suited: [blog / video / podcast / social / webinar]

5. FUNNEL COVERAGE
   TOFU content: formats and topics for awareness stage
   MOFU content: formats and topics for consideration stage
   BOFU content: formats and topics for decision stage
   Gap analysis: where the current coverage is weakest

6. FORMAT MIX
   For each format in the mix:
   Format | Production effort | Distribution channel | Funnel stage | Frequency

7. DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS
   Owned: blog, email newsletter, social profiles, podcast
   Earned: PR, guest content, organic search
   Paid: content amplification, sponsored distribution
   For each channel: role in the strategy and KPI

8. EDITORIAL CALENDAR STRUCTURE
   Publishing cadence by format.
   Lead time requirements by format.
   Seasonal or event-driven content windows.
   Content refresh schedule for evergreen pieces.

9. CONTENT OPERATIONS
   Roles: who produces, edits, approves, and distributes.
   Workflow: from brief to publish.
   Tools: CMS, project management, SEO, analytics.
   Briefing standard: reference seo-brief.md for SEO content.

10. MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK
    Primary metric: the one number defining content success.
    By funnel stage:
    TOFU: organic traffic, social reach, newsletter subscribers
    MOFU: content downloads, webinar registrations, time on page
    BOFU: content-influenced pipeline, content-attributed conversions
    Reporting cadence and owner.
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a content strategist writing a company content strategy.

Company: [name and what it does]
Target audience: [primary and secondary audiences]
Business objective: [what business goal content must support]
Current content situation: [what exists, what is working, what is not]
Competitive content landscape: [what competitors are doing well]
Resources: [team size, publishing capacity, tools available]
Time horizon: [this strategy covers what period]

Produce a complete content strategy covering all 10 sections.
Content pillars must sit at the intersection of audience need, brand authority, and business relevance.
The funnel coverage section must identify where the current mix is weakest.
Every format in the mix must have a stated role, frequency, and KPI.
```
