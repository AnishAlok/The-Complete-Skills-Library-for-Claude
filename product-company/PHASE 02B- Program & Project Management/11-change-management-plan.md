# change-management-plan.md

> **Skill:** Produces a change management plan that assesses the people impact of a product or organizational change, identifies affected groups, plans communications and enablement, and tracks adoption.

---

## TLDR

Change management is the discipline of helping people adopt changes successfully. A change management plan answers: *"Who is affected by this change, how significantly, and what do we need to do to help them adopt it successfully?"* Used when a product change, process change, or organizational change will affect how people work and requires active management of the human transition.

---

## Topic Explanation

### What Change Management Is Not

Change management is frequently confused with project management or communications planning. Key distinctions:

**Project management:** Manages the delivery of the change (scope, timeline, resources). "Did we build and release the new feature?"

**Communications planning:** Manages information dissemination. "Did we tell people about the change?"

**Change management:** Manages human adoption of the change. "Are people actually using the new feature and have they successfully transitioned away from the old way of working?"

All three are necessary for successful organizational change. Change management specifically addresses the gap between "we shipped it" and "people are actually using it differently."

### The ADKAR Model (Prosci)

The ADKAR model provides the foundational individual change framework:
- **Awareness:** Do people know the change is happening and why?
- **Desire:** Do people want to change?
- **Knowledge:** Do people know how to change?
- **Ability:** Can people actually make the change?
- **Reinforcement:** Are there mechanisms to sustain the change over time?

A change management plan should address all five ADKAR elements. The most common failure: organizations invest heavily in Awareness (communications) and Knowledge (training) but neglect Desire (stakeholder engagement), Ability (hands-on practice and support), and Reinforcement (sustained adoption measurement).

### Resistance Is Normal

Resistance to change is not a sign that the change is wrong. It is a normal human response to disruption of established patterns. Change management plans should anticipate resistance, identify its likely sources, and build in specific strategies to address it rather than ignoring it or treating it as a problem to be overcome by force.

## Output Format

```
1. CHANGE OVERVIEW
   What is changing: description of the change.
   Why it is changing: the business rationale.
   Timeline: when the change takes effect.
   Scope: what is changing vs. what is staying the same.

2. CHANGE IMPACT ASSESSMENT
   For each affected group:
   - Group name and size
   - How they currently work (current state)
   - How they will work after the change (future state)
   - Impact level: High / Medium / Low
   - What they must stop doing
   - What they must start doing
   - What they must do differently

3. STAKEHOLDER ANALYSIS
   For each stakeholder group:
   - Role in the change: sponsor / target / influencer / resistor
   - Current change readiness: supportive / neutral / resistant
   - Key concerns
   - Engagement strategy

4. ADKAR PLAN
   For each affected group:
   Awareness: how will they learn about the change and why?
   Desire: how will we build motivation to change?
   Knowledge: what training and documentation is needed?
   Ability: what practice, support, and tools are needed?
   Reinforcement: how will adoption be sustained and measured?

5. COMMUNICATIONS PLAN
   For each message / audience / channel:
   - Message: what they need to know
   - Audience: specific group
   - Channel: email / meeting / in-product / manager cascade
   - Timing: before / during / after change
   - Owner

6. TRAINING AND ENABLEMENT PLAN
   Training required by group.
   Format, delivery method, timing.
   Who delivers it.

7. RESISTANCE MANAGEMENT
   Anticipated sources of resistance.
   Root causes of each.
   Specific strategies to address each.

8. ADOPTION MEASUREMENT
   How adoption will be measured.
   Success metrics and targets.
   Timeline for measuring adoption milestones.
   What triggers additional support vs. declaring success.
```

## Starter Prompt

```
You are a change manager planning a change management approach.

The change: [what is changing - product, process, or organizational]
Why it is changing: [the business rationale]
Affected groups: [who will be affected and approximately how many]
Timeline: [when the change takes effect]
Known concerns or resistance: [any resistance already identified]
Available channels: [how you can communicate with affected groups]
Measurement capability: [what adoption data you can track]

Produce a complete change management plan covering all ADKAR elements
for each affected group. Include communications plan, training plan,
resistance management strategies, and adoption measurement approach.
```
